# Taiwan Milk Market Analysis

## Summary (English)
Milk prices in Taiwan are significantly higher than in most developed markets.  
Despite trade liberalization and the introduction of zero-tariff imports, retail prices remain elevated.  

This repository organizes publicly available data and observations to explain:
- Why prices remain high  
- Why imports have limited impact  
- How market structure influences outcomes  

---

## Key Observations

- Taiwan retail milk price: ~90–120 TWD/L  
- Import cost (CIF): ~30–40 TWD/L  
- Domestic production: ~450,000 tons/year  
- Liquid milk imports: ~50,000 tons/year  

→ Imports account for roughly **10% of total liquid milk supply**

---

## Core Insight: Volume Constraint

Even if imported milk is cheaper,  
**its total volume is too small to significantly affect the overall market price.**

- Domestic supply dominates (~90%)
- Imports remain limited (~10%)

→ Price competition is structurally constrained

---

## Market Dynamics

### 1. Retail Pricing Behavior
- Imported milk is not always sold cheaper at retail level  
- Price differences between domestic and imported products are sometimes reduced or reversed  

→ Retail channels influence final consumer price significantly  

---

### 2. Demand-side Response
- Some consumers attempt to switch to imported milk  
- In certain cases, increased demand leads to price increases in imported products  

→ Consumer substitution alone may not be sufficient to drive system-wide price change  

---

### 3. Policy Influence
- Programs that increase consumption (e.g., subsidized milk distribution)  
- May stabilize demand regardless of price  

→ Demand support can reduce downward price pressure

## Common Misconceptions

### ❌ Taiwan domestic milk is fresh milk because processed by lower temperature? (台灣鮮奶殺菌溫度較低所以比較新鮮比較好?)
**Fact：**
- Most milk(~90%) on market uses UHT processing(~135 celsius degree).
- But it can only perserved no more than 13 days under 7 celsius degree
- Shelf life difference mainly comes from packaging and marketing strategy.

---

### ❌ Imported milk lasts longer because of preservatives
**Fact:**
- Long shelf life is typically achieved through:
  - Ultra-High Temperature (UHT) processing  
  - Aseptic packaging  
- No preservatives are required under standard production  

---

### ❌ Shorter shelf life = higher freshness
**Fact:**
- Shelf life depends on processing, packaging, and distribution  
- Not solely an indicator of freshness or quality  

---

### ❌ Taiwan milk is expensive only because of cost
**Fact:**
- Cost is one factor  
- Market structure, supply control, and retail pricing also play major roles  

---

## Structural Constraints

Price reduction requires **all three conditions**:

1. Sufficient low-cost supply  
2. Adequate market access  
3. Consumer acceptance  

If any of these are limited → price remains elevated  

---

## Comparison to Other Markets

Products such as:
- Diamonds  
- Caviar  

Have experienced price shifts due to:
- Increased production capacity  
- Technological improvements  
- Large-scale market entry  

→ Key factor: **sufficient volume + market acceptance**

## Implication

In the Taiwan milk market:

- Import supply is still limited  
- Market structure restricts price transmission  
- Consumer behavior alone cannot overcome structural constraints  

→ Price stability persists despite external competition
