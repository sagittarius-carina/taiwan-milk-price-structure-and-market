# Milk Market Myths (Taiwan)

## Myth 1: Imported milk contains preservatives

**Claim:**  
Imported milk lasts longer, so it must contain preservatives.

**Fact:**  
- Shelf life is achieved through Ultra-High Temperature (UHT) processing  
- Aseptic packaging prevents contamination  
- No preservatives are required under standard production  

**Conclusion:**  
Long shelf life does not imply added preservatives.

---

## Myth 2: Shorter shelf life means fresher milk

**Claim:**  
Milk that spoils faster is more natural or fresher.

**Fact:**  
- Shelf life depends on processing and packaging  
- Both short and long shelf life milk can meet safety standards  
- Freshness is not determined solely by shelf life  

**Conclusion:**  
Shelf life alone is not a reliable indicator of freshness.

---

## Myth 3: High price is purely due to production cost

**Claim:**  
Taiwan milk is expensive only because production cost is high.

**Fact:**  
- Import cost (CIF) is significantly lower  
- Retail pricing varies across channels  
- Market structure affects price transmission  

**Conclusion:**  
Price is influenced by multiple factors beyond production cost.

---

## Myth 4: Imports will automatically lower prices

**Claim:**  
Introducing imports will reduce domestic prices.

**Fact:**  
- Import volume is limited (~10%)  
- Domestic supply dominates the market  
- Price impact depends on total available volume  

**Conclusion:**  
Imports alone cannot significantly reduce prices without sufficient scale.
